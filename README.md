# 🏠 Room-Wise UPS-Integrated Residential Power System

## Overview
This project presents the design, analysis, and simulation of a **Room-Wise 
UPS-Integrated Residential Electrical Power System**, developed as a **Complex 
Engineering Problem (CEP)** for the EE-111 Circuit Analysis 1 course at 
**PIEAS, Islamabad**.

The project models a complete residential house with **11 rooms** and **13 types 
of household appliances** including fans, LED bulbs, AC inverters, refrigerators, 
washing machines, water pumps, and more. All rooms are connected in **parallel** 
to ensure equal voltage distribution at the standard Pakistani supply of **230V**.

Using **MATLAB**, all electrical parameters — resistance, current, and power — 
were calculated component-wise, room-wise, and for the total house. The complete 
wiring circuit along with **UPS switching logic** was designed and simulated in 
**NI Multisim 14.2**, validating circuit behavior under both main supply and 
backup power conditions. House layout and professional diagrams were created 
using **Canva**.

The total connected load was determined to be **8945W**, with UPS-supported 
essential devices consuming **1395W**. A **2000W UPS** was selected to ensure 
reliable backup, surge handling, and future flexibility.
